COURSE DESCRIPTION
This course provides an overview of the fundamental concepts and issues related to urban environment that planners face and introduces students to the multitude of topics associated with this field, especially sustainable development. Major topics include: conceptual frameworks of environmental planning and policy; environmental justice, equity and collaborative planning; planning for contemporary environmental challenges (solid and hazardous waste; water quality, air quality; climate change; natural disasters and hazard mitigation); environmental impact assessment; growth management and sustainability; environmental preservation and conservation techniques; low impact and green infrastructure and development. 

COURSE OBJECTIVES
As part of this course, students will be able to:
1. understand the underlying factors that shaped the environmental planning and policymaking movement;
2. understand the current environmental issues and challenges and identify the character and nature of these challenges;
3. identify the major environmental planning-related issues associated with environmental topics such as: environmental justice; air quality; water quality and wastewater management; climate change; disaster planning; the built and natural environment; low impact and green development.
4. analyze and evaluate planning and programs to address environmental challenges and promote sustainable development.
