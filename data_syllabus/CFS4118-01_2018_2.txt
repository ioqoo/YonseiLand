Syllabus_Korean
##
- The goal of this course is to examine various influences on a child`s development and socialization by focusing on individual child factors (biological, socio-emotional, cognitive); the role of parents, sibling and the extended family, as well as the broader social contexts (e.g., schools and peers). This course will also a) examine historical and theoretical foundations of parenting research and practice; b) explore cultural and contextual factors influencing parenthood and parent-child relationships; c) evaluate changes in parenting styles and parenting strategies across developmental stages; and d) examine current research on parenting and parent-child relationships.
- Students are challenged to integrate theory, research findings, and public policy as they consider a variety of issues related to parenting and the parent-child relationship.
