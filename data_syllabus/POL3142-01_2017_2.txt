##
First half of the class takes an historical as well as analytical approach to international political economy. The students are expected to understand major theories of IPE. Second half of the class will deal with current issues of IPE in the post-cold war era: issues such as globalization, economic development, trade and protectionism, MNCs and global production, international monetary relations. The course is on political and economical aspects of international political economy. 

Major themes to be discussed in the class are the following:
* Theories of International Political Economy
* State and Market 
* Economic Development
* Economic Crisis
* Globalization & Regionalism 
* International finance & monetary order
* International trade
* E-economy
