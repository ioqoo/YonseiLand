Syllabus_Korean
##
This course will cover variety of different issues in the context of law and practice of international business transactions.   
The central goal of this course is to familiarize students with a broad range of analytical and legal tools to enable them to think and act critically when they will play roles in the fields of international business transactions.

Syllabus_English
##
This course will cover variety of different issues related with international business transactions. This course will provide an introduction to the method and substance of how to review international contracts and agreements.  
We will further explore areas of international disputes resolution including international arbitration and the Investor State Dispute Settlement (ISDS). This course will cover the UN Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (Vienna, 1980) (“CISG”), Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards (New York, 1958) (the “New York Convention”), the International Chamber of Commerce (“ICC”) and the ICC rules for the use of domestic and international trade terms (“Incoterms”), etc.  
In addition, this course will cover comprehensive comparison and analysis of substantive laws on receivables financing transactions of major jurisdictions, encompassing common law jurisdictions, Roman-Germanic jurisdictions and French-Napoleonic jurisdictions as well as relevant EU Directives and relevant international conventions.
