Syllabus_Korean
##
This course serves as a junior-level undergraduate introduction to the study of international security. The goal of the course is to familiarize students with basic concepts and theories to understand contemporary debates and issues in national and international security affairs. The syllabus is organized in 12 thematic sections (that exclude Chuseok Holiday, Midterm and Final Exam). First, we will study basic concepts, theories and logics that are useful for making sense of contemporary security issues. Second, we will survey several important issues in national and international security with frequent use of recent historical examples. By the end of the class, students will be able to have acquired the analytical tools to begin their own academic and policy research.
