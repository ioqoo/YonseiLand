Syllabus_English
##
PHY3101-01 Quantum Mechanics (1)

Spring, 2017

Classes: 3 hrs of lectures: Mon 5,6, Wed 5 (Wed 6)

Instructor: Kyungsun Moon (kmoon@yonsei.ac.kr) 
Office: Science Hall 342 

Text: 
D. J. Griffiths, "Introduction to Quantum Mechanics", Prentice Hall (1994)

Supplementary texts: 
S. Gasiorowicz, "Quantum Physics", John Wiley & Sons
R. L. Liboff, "Introductory Quantum Mechanics", Addison-Wesley

Grading: Midterm exam:  100 %
         Final Exam:    100 %
         Homework:       40 %
         Class Participation:    20 %

Course Objectives:
Develop an understanding of modern concepts of quantum mechanics: 
Basic principles, the Schroedinger equation,
wave functions, and physical interpretation. Bound and continuum states 
in one-dimensional systems. Feynman path integrals.
Quantum harmonic oscillator; coherent states.
Bound states in central potential; hydrogen atom.
Rotations and angular momentum. Other symmetries.
