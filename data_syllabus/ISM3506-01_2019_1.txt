Syllabus_Korean
##
This course discusses many of the current debates and questions in the scholarship related to conflict and cooperation in the international system. The first half of the course focuses on the theoretical bases on state interaction and war, particularly the literature on prospects on cooperation, security organizations, and armed violence. The latter half of the course explores a broad array of recent topics pertaining to international security, ranging from socioeconomic inequality to gender issues. 

The objectives of this course is to enable students to: a) become fluent in the emerging literature related to security, peace, and conflict; and b) write a research paper on topics on international security.
