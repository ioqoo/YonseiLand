Syllabus_Korean
##
본 수업에서는 민주주의의 질에 중요한 영향을 미치는 국가 관료제의 역사적 기원과 최근의 변화에 대한 핵심적인 개념과 이론적 틀을 학습한다. 한국 사례에 초점을 맞추되, 다양한 비교 사례에 대한 학습을 통해 균형적인 시각을 함양하도록 한다. 궁극적으로는 ‘관료제와 민주주의’ 이론과 실제를 통합적으로 학습함으로써, 한국적 상황에서 민주적 관료제를 구현하기 위한 조건과 방안을 모색하는 데 학습 목표를 둔다. 이번 학기에 본 수업에서 다루고자 하는 주요 연구 질문은 다음과 같다: 공직(희망)자의 공직 입직 동기는 공적인가 사적인가? 행정엘리트로 이루어진 관료제가 민주주의에 순기능을 할 것인가?

Syllabus_English
##
This course explores how public bureaucracy has emerged and evolved in modern democracies, and any interested students are free to join. There are no formal prerequisites for this course although background knowledge of public administration, political science, and/or the Korean government and politics is recommended.  
This course is intended to introduce fundamental concepts and framework for understanding how recent changes in state bureaucracy affect the quality of democracy. It will also examine theoretical and empirical implications of various forms of bureaucracy with special attention to Korean cases. To be more specific, a series of lectures will offer a survey of major institutions, actors, and the decision-making process of multiple bureaucratic systems. Ultimately, students will gain not only comprehensive knowledge of democratic bureaucracy, but also critical thinking skills to make a policy suggestion on bureaucratic reforms which will be especially helpful for those who plan to work in the (quasi-) public sector.
Although this course does not require students to have prior knowledge, some students may want to get a general idea about major theories and perspectives pertaining to bureaucracy and democracy. The following books will help you acquire a basic familiarity on the topic. You don’t have to purchase them unless if you want. You can find all other required and recommended readings on electoral reserve:

> 장훈/이승주. (2018). 한국형 발전국가의 정치경제사 (인간사랑).
> 김성희. (2018). 너는 검정 (창비). 
> B. Guy Peters. (2010). The Politics of Bureaucracy: An Introduction to Comparative Public Administration 6th ed. (Abingdon; New York: Routledge).
> James Q. Wilson. (1989). Bureaucracy: What Government Agencies Do and Why They Do It (New York: Basic Books).
