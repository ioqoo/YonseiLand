COURSE DESCRIPTION:
This course offers a multidisciplinary approach to the study of the United States in the world. It explores the interactions between America and the rest of the world in major historical junctures since the Cold War.  
The course also analyzes the important postwar economic and political institutions and order created by the United States and other countries to meet the challenges of a globalized world economy and politics.  The course ends with discussions and analyses of the current Trump administration`s policies and discover the domestic foundations of American power in the world.

LEARNING OBJECTIVES:
- Stimulate critical analysis of current issues and debates in international relations and US foreign policy;
- Explore the historical, political and social foundations of policy and practice in the United States;
- Identify and discuss the images of the United States in the world 

* Each student will participate in weekly discussions on the US presidential election and important incidents and issues.  It is important for you to keep abreast of recent issues by reading The New York Times, The Economist, and/or Financial Times, as well as other main media sources.
