##
In the years between the end of the Second World War and the Brexit referendum, “the empire-state” as David Cannadine suggests, “was superseded by the much-diminished island-state”. This decline was a complex affair, since while the Empire was effectively wound up with the handover of Hong Kong to China in 1997, living standards in the United Kingdom continued to rise. One of the main reasons for this was that the UK had done a remarkably good job at selling itself to the world: the island-state had successfully replaced the British Empire with the wholesale global marketing of its major cultural attractions. The course will conclude with some thoughts about whether the model of the post-war multicultural society is now experiencing a form of delayed social crisis,
