DISCLAIMER: This is a TENTATIVE online syllabus subject to change prior to the 
beginning of the course. A more detailed syllabus will be provided on the first 
day of class.

This course will provide the framework for the career decision making process. 
It stresses the connection between the student’s chosen academic field and 
career objectives. Topics include: exploring options  after graduation, making 
career choices, self-assessment, employment opportunities, graduate school 
applications, creating an effective résumé, job interview techniques, and a 
résumé review and critique. Assignments, discussions and writing exercises will 
be conducted both in class and online (YSCEC). Students are expected to 
actively participate in both formats.

The focus of this course is to provide students with the 
professional writing and interviewing skills that are required for employment 
or graduate school. By the end of this course you will learn how to do the 
following:
1. produce clear, measurable and practical personal and career goals 
2. identify personal interests, strengths and weaknesses, and relate 
those to your career goals 
3. create essays for graduate school applications
4. describe and explore career paths and options 
5. employ techniques for job-search activities, such as developing 
resumes and interviewing skills 
6. develop and present a professional portfolio for use in job 
interviews
