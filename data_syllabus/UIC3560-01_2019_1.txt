Syllabus_Korean
##
The adaptation of modern corporate structures and methods in Japan in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries was a pivotal event in the history of world business. This course, "Modernizing Japanese Business as a Social Phenomenon," examines these events in terms of how they were experienced by various groups of business stakeholders  including employees, managers, and consumers. process by which businesses in Japan underwent the transition from native practices to models influenced by modern techniques. We will focus particularly on such topics as management, finance, labor relations, international linkages, and consumer culture.
