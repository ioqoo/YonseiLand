COURSE DESCRIPTION: In this course, we will approach writing as a process to 
help you discover the strategies that work best for you in order to become a 
confident writer. We will build on skills you already have and introduce you 
to the conventions of academic writing and critical thinking to help you 
succeed as writers in college: summarizing, critiquing, analyzing, and 
synthesizing. Emphasis will be placed on format, organization, development, 
coherence, unity of focus, and consideration of audience and purpose. The 
reading, writing, revising, and editing skills you develop in this course will 
help you throughout your academic career, regardless of your major.

COURSE GOALS: By the end of this course, you will learn how to: 
1. summarize and critique an argument
2. demonstrate thoughtful interaction with the work of others through 
analysis and logical use of evidence
3. integrate sources with each other and with oneâ€™s own ideas
4. demonstrate the ability to provide constructive feedback to peers
5. demonstrate knowledge of genre conventions in terms of organization, 
formatting, and paragraphing
6. demonstrate understanding of the purposes and conventions of MLA 
documentation
